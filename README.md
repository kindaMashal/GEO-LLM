# Graph-based Evaluation framework for Optimization via LLMs

Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly being used to generate mathematical formulations for optimization problems. However, the formulations generated may include a variety of mistakes, and a simple short example a mistake can be: a generated formulation may miss certain constraints, introduce redundant constraints, or split or merge some constraints. However, generating an incorrect mathematical formula doesn't only stop at the contraints, but can also generate incorrect objective functions or produce an unwated variable level differences. 

Methods and metrics to evaluate the mathematical formula generated by LLMs have been made before but all have its shortcomings and disadvantages. Therefore, we are researching this new approach to evaluate the mathematical formula generated by LLMs using graphs to represent the formulas, and graphs similarity methods so measure the difference between the ground through and the generated formula.
